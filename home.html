Hiking Essentials

Before setting off on your first big hike, it is vital to bring the right gear with you for your journey. A big walk might not seem like a challenge, but long stretches of excercise coupled with Ireland's almost random weather patterns could turn your walk into a miserable trek in minutes. Use our checklist below to ensure you are ready for any elements the Emerald Isle may throw at you!

Should you need to purchase anything from the list below, visit Amazon here!

Essential Items

  • Waterproof Backpack

    A must have for any hike, as you'll need it to carry any supplies with you and keep them dry.

  • Hiking Boots

    A decent pair of boots will keep your feet comfortable and provide a decent grip for any uphill climbs.

  • Waterproof Clothing

    Ireland is known for spontaneous showers and damp conditions. Keep yourself warm with waterproof outer layers, like a raincoat and waterproof pants.

  • Food/Drink

    Always pack a meal and water for any hike. Chances are you won't run into a supermarket halfway up a mountain, so keep yourself fed and hydrated.

Optional Items

  • Walking Pole/Stick

    A walking pole can help take unnecessary pressure from your body when hiking and provides extra stability on steep climbs. A decent stick can function just as well if there are some nearby.

  • Sunglasses + Sunscreen

    If the weather is particularly kind on your hike day, bring sunglasses and sunscreen with you. A gorgeous hike in the sun can be spoiled if you get sun burnt and peel like a potatoe when you return home.

  • First Aid Kit

    When hiking particularly steep or dangerous areas, pack a first aid kit. If an accident occurs, you may need to apply medical attention to an injury.

  • Extra Phone Battery

    If you're going on any long hike, an extra phone battery never hurts. You may need to view maps of your area or call someone in a pinch, so keep your phone topped up on battery life.

  • Sleeping Bag/Tent

    If you're going on any long hike, an extra phone battery never hurts. You may need to view maps of your area or call someone in a pinch, so keep your phone topped up on battery life.

  • Multi-tool Knife

    Should you decide to make camp, a Multi-tool will prove invaluable to you. A multi-tool will have blades needed to collecting wood for a campfire, a decent multi-tool will also have a firestarting kit built in.
